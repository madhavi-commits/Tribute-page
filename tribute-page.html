Ratan Naval Tata

Indian industrialist, philanthropist and former chairman of Tata Sons.

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was a chairman of the Tata Group from 1990 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017.

Here's a time line of Ratan Naval Tata life:

  • 1937-Ratan Tata was born in Bombay, now Mumbai, during the British Raj, on 28 December 1937.
  • 1948- when Tata was 10, his parents separated, and he was subsequently raised and adopted by Navajbai Tata, his grandmother and widow of Ratanji Tata.
  • 1959-After graduating from high school, Tata enrolled in Cornell University, from which he graduated with a bachelor's degree in architecture in 1959.
  • 1970-In the 1970s, Tata was given a managerial position in the Tata group.
  • 2008- In 2008, Tata gifted Cornell $50 Million, becoming the largest international donor in the university's history.
  • 2014-In 2014, Tata Group endowed the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay and formed the Tata Center for Technology and Design (TCTD) to develop design and engineering principles suited to the needs of people and communities with limited resources.
